Fig 5.7.1. Open circuit characteristics (OCC) of alternator
Schematic electrical connection diagram for experimentally determining the OCC is
shown in Fig 5.7.2. Keeping the output terminals open, the machine is run at
constant rated speed through the prime mover. The field current (If) is increased in
steps and the corresponding terminal voltages (Vto) which also happen to be the
induced EMF (Ef), are recorded to plot the OCC curve. It is necessary that the speed
be held at constant rated value during the experiment since any deviations will result
in change in the induced EMF value thereby introducing error in measurement.
Fig 5.7.2 Connection diagram for experimentally determining OCC of alternator
Procedure for OCC:
1. Make connections as shown in Figure 5.7.2.
2. With field excitation of the alternator switched off, start the set with the help of
the DC motor and bring the speed of the set to rated speed of the alternator.
3. Keep the field regulator of the field circuit of the alternator-at zero position. Take
the reading of the terminal voltage of the alternator (open circuit voltage of the
alternator).
4. Increase the alternator field current gradually in steps by the field regulator and
at each step take the readings of the field current and terminal voltage of the
alternator. At every step the alternator speed should be kept constant at rated
speed. Take readings up to 120% of rated alternator voltage.
5. Switch off the circuit.

Fig 5.7.3.Short circuit characteristics (SCC) of the alternator
Schematic connection diagram for experimental determination of SCC is shown in
Fig 5.7.4. The field current If is raised from zero to an amount sufficient to circulate
a short- circuit current which is 125 to 150 percent of rated armature current of the
machine. Since the output load is shorted, a very small amount of EMF is sufficient
to drive rated current through the synchronous reactance of the alternator. Thus, the
field current required to produce such small amount of EMF is also quite small
during the short circuit test. This prevents saturation of the magnetic parts during
short circuit test. So the SCC is a straight line graph.
Fig 5.7.4. Schematic connection diagram for experimental determination of SCC
Procedure for SCC:
1. Make connections as shown in Figure 5.7.4.
2. With field excitation of the alternator switched off, adjust the speed of the motor
to rated value.
3. Keep the field circuit field regulator of the alternator in zero position initially.
Gradually increase the field current in steps till rated current flows through the
armature winding of the alternator. Take 6-8 readings.
4. Switch off the circuit.
