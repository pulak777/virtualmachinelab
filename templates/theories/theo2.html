The above expressions indicates that the speed Nr of an induction motor can be controlled by
varying the synchronous speed NS or the slip s.
The synchronous speed can be changed by varying either the frequency f, or the number of
poles P of the motor. On the other hand, the slip s can be controlled by varying the supply
voltage, or by varying the motor circuit resistance or reactance. Accordingly, the practical
methods of speed control of a 3-phase induction motor can be listed as:
(a) Variation of supply voltage
(b) Variation of supply frequency
(c) Variation of number of poles
(d) Variation of rotor circuit resistance or reactance
Since poles can only be integers, speed control by pole changing method only provides
discrete steps of speed variation. One of the simplest methods of controlling the speed is thus
by varying the supply voltage.
